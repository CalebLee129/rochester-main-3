We are the University of Rochester: Cyanovolt

Our team is composed of 11 undergrads passionate about STEM and using science to bring about positive change in our community. Our team comprises of members from sophomores to seniors with different majors. We are guided by a group of advisors, including head advisor Dr. Anne Meyer, who has led many previous iGEM teams to grand prizes and notable awards. Everyone on this team embodies the spirit and ideals of iGEM. We are excited to see how we can utilize science to bring about positive change in our community.

  • Our project combines clean energy and sustainability to contribute to stopping the climate crisis through the creation of a CO2-sequestering bio-photovoltaic cell powered by modified cyanobacteria.
  • Electrons released while photosynthesis occurs in the cyanobacteria can be picked up by the photovoltaic cell and redirected to create electrical output, thus giving us a source of green energy.
  • We will modify our strain of cyanobacteria by targeting a carbon-fixing enzymes to increase the effectiveness and efficiency in CO2 sequestration of our chassis.
  • Alongside electricity production and CO2 sequestration, we will engineer our cyanobacteria to synthesize a high- value product, such as ethanol.
  • The cyanobacteria will be screenprinted onto an inexpensive paper substrate, maximizing the total surface area of the cyanobacterial culture and effectively producing a cheap and sustainable “solar panel” without the need for expensive materials.
