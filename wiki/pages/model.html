Overview

We created several modeling projects to support the wet lab and the development of the hardware. The modeling projects focus on the most important parts of the project which include the ability of the cyanobacteria to sequester carbon dioxide from the environment, and the ability for the cyanobacteria to generate electricity.

The Main goals of our model:

  1. To aid the hardware team in designing the most optimal pattern of electrodes for the biophotovoltaic cell to result in the maximum output of electricity.
  2. To provide information to wet lab about the ability of the wild type and modified cyanobacteria to sequester carbon dioxide.

Carbon Sequestration Model

Background

Goals

This model aims to provide more information about two questions which are explored in the Wet Lab:

  1. How does the overexpression of SbtA and BicA in cyanobacteria affect the efficiency of carbon sequestration?
  2. How does replacing the Wild Type Carbonic Anhydrase with N. vulgaris Carbonic Anhydrase in cyanobacteria affect the efficiency of carbon sequestration?
